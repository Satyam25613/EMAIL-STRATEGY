The Post-Signup Philosophy

This document is our core playbook for communicating with KaraX users across their entire lifecycle.

The big goal: We want to eliminate "marketing speak" entirely. Users shouldn't feel like they are being watched, graded, or nudged through an artificial software funnel. The second an email reads like "We noticed you haven't logged in...", the user puts their guard up.

Instead, we are adopting Serendipitous Professionalism. Our emails must arrive as universally helpful, incredibly human, and hyper-relevant capability suggestions. It should feel less like dealing with a software company, and more like getting a quick tip from a brilliant executive assistant.

Sequence 01

The Activation Catalyst

When they get this
Fired 30 minutes after they sign up, only if they sign up and do actually nothing.
What we want to achieve
Break through the hesitation of the "blank canvas." Most people don't know what to ask an AI first. We want to hand them an immediate win.

Why this works brilliantly

We know they signed up and did nothing. But instead of calling them out on it, we send a genuinely empathetic tip about the concept of "starting simple" and offer them an immediate, fast shortcut. It feels like guidance, not nagging.

A quick tip for your first day
"Welcome to KaraX. I know exploring a new tool can sometimes feel a bit overwhelming, especially when you're starting from scratch..."

Sequence 03

The Upgrade Pitch

When they get this
Triggered right after they complete their 5th active workflow on the free tier.
What we want to achieve

Upgrades often feel like unfair paywalls. Instead, we reframe the paid tier as an architectural choice. We tell them: "You're clearly scaling. Upgrading means you don't even have to log in anymore."

Sequence 04

Silent Re-engagement

We completely ignore the fact that they've been gone. Instead, we just show up in their inbox carrying tools that make their current week easier. We resurrect their interest purely through convenience.
